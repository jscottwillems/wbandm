About Us



WB&M got started back in 1981 by Ann and Gene Salge. Originally the business was in the Waterbed and furniture business, hence the acronym, WB&M for Water Beds and More. The More portion has continued in a big way! Gene Salge, a Vietnam War Veteran, was the woodworking mastermind and process improvement specialist that took raw wood into his shop and spit out finished goods. Those goods were then shipped to the retail location where Ann Salge would manage and sell to the public. The first retail location was in the heart of Humble, TX in the old Post Office building on Higgins Street, while the manufacturing location was setup behind the residence of the Salge family in New Caney, TX. Disaster struck the manufacturing plant just before Christmas in 1985 when an electrical fan malfunctioned and caught fire. The entire shop was lost. Perseverance is a Salge trait and was on display as the shop was rebuilt. The Retail location made several moves over the years from the Post Office building over to the old Academy shopping center near the railroad tracks on 1960 Bypass. Later in early 90s, the location moved to 1st street near famous Trigg’s Restaurant and then in late 90s it moved to its current location at the corner of 1st Street and Wilson Road. As the Waterbed craze started winding down in the late 80’s and early 90’s, Ann and Gene looked to the future and saw the computer revolution coming. Gene loved being able to design things and have computer controlled devices build, cut, and create new items. He started working on computerized Embroidery. The More in Waterbeds and More took on a new meaning. The Embroidery business started off with 1 machine that Gene and Casey (Ann & Gene’s only son) took to different events and craft shows starting in 1992. Eventually there was enough demand that the Embroidery side of the business debuted in the Retail location in 1994. For over 25+ years, the Salge family has been providing their customers with a superior product at competitive prices. In 2015, Casey and his beautiful bride Carrie, purchased the business from Ann & Gene and continued the legacy that was established in 1981. We welcome you to our location and we are honored to continue offering the MORE in WB&M. With Embroidery and Screen Printing being the most popular services, we also offer banners, thermal film, just about any imprinting that you would like on anything that you would like. A new CNC Laser will be added to the line up in early 2019 along with additional promotional products. Finally, we would like to thank the community for trusting in us for all of these years and continuing to allow us to earn your business. Stop on by and have a beverage with us on the couch and let’s catch up.

At WB&M our customers remain foremost in our plans and goals. From the initial consultation, through the design phase and final production, we realize that each new project must reflect our customer’s specific needs. We pledge to understand and honor those individual requirements and to bring customer satisfaction with every finished product. Personal service and attention to detail are the prescribed methods of our way of business. We will ensure that each of our customers leaves satisfied with a quality product.

Our WB&M team strives to provide a safe, clean and comfortable work environment for our employees. Our goal is to produce screen printing, embroidery, vinyl graphic and other products that are of a consistent level of high standards. We will continue to maintain our equipment, purchase new items when needed and perform regular inspections to maintain our top quality levels. We strive to increase our business with future orders without compromise to our customers or employees needs.
